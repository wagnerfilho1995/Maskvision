Summary

Understanding the behavior of the optical amplifier in different situations is a crucial point for the correct use of this device in optical networks. Factors such as amplifier type, input power, gain and number of channels, influence the amplifier's behavior. Most companies that carry out optical network planning use an old model in which the amplifier's behavior is constant, regardless of the factors mentioned above. However, in a dynamic environment, this simplistic consideration makes network planning even further from implementation. Therefore, the objective of this work is to develop a software that allows the modeling and visualization of the amplifier's behavior, considering the variation of this behavior according to the factors that influence it. Such software, when used in the context of optical network planning, will make the simulation and optimization process much more faithful to reality, allowing for more efficient planning. Therefore, since the correct configuration of the optical amplifier depends on a good understanding of its behavior, and that this device has an impact on the performance of the optical network as a whole, the use of this software in companies that carry out optical network planning (ex: Cisco, Nokia Networks and PadTec) will result in better results in terms of performance and cost.



The Maskvision

Optical amplifiers are extremely important equipment for the operation of the optical network, as they compensate for the loss of power that the signal suffers during transmission, maintaining the power of the channels at levels suitable for the reception. On the other hand, the optical amplifier can also degrade the signal by changing the signal format and inserting noise. Therefore, it is necessary to correctly define the point of operation of these devices so that the quality of the transmission is enhanced. For this definition to happen efficiently, it is essential that the points at which the amplifier can operate are well defined, that is, that there is accurate modeling of the amplifier's behavior in different scenarios. Currently, this modeling considers that the amplifiers have the same behavior in different scenarios.

Therefore, more precise modeling is necessary, considering a larger volume of information, thus making the creation of a model a more complex task.

The general objective of this project is to present a software that enables more detailed and correct visualization and modeling of the optical amplifier operation.

A software that assists in the modeling and projection of good behavior of optical amplifiers is essential to design optical networks better than those that currently exist. Therefore, we present Maskvision as a tool that will help the development of future optical communication networks.
